Smart Contract

A smart contract is code on a blockchain that runs automatically when rules are met. It enforces agreements without intermediaries, using transparent, tamper-resistant logic.

In detail

A smart contract is a program stored on a blockchain that executes predefined logic when specific conditions are met. Because the contract’s code and state are recorded on a distributed ledger, its behavior is transparent, verifiable, and difficult to tamper with. Smart contracts can hold assets, enforce permissions, trigger events, and interact with other contracts and off-chain services through oracles. They are used to automate agreements, reduce manual processes, and increase trust between parties who may not know each other. Proper testing, security reviews, and upgrade strategies are critical to prevent bugs and vulnerabilities.

Synonyms

self-executing contract, on-chain contract
