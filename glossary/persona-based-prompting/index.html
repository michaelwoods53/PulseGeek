Persona-Based Prompting

A strategy that assigns the model a role, audience, and tone to shape style and decisions. It improves consistency for marketing, support, and teaching tasks across channels and formats.

In detail

Persona-based prompting directs a model by defining a clear role, voice, and audience context. You describe who the model is, what it values, and how it should communicate, such as a helpful support agent or a senior marketer. The persona includes tone, reading level, and domain boundaries, which creates more consistent outputs across tasks and channels. Effective personas also include do and do not lists, brand language rules, and examples of preferred phrasing. To prevent drift, you can add guardrails like banned claims, safety constraints, and required references. Persona prompts pair well with structured templates, schema outputs, and evaluation rubrics so you can score style, accuracy, and brand compliance. They are often tested with A/B experiments and used in zero-shot or few-shot setups. Risks include overfitting to style at the expense of factual accuracy, or leaking confidential brand guidance in public contexts, which you can reduce with review workflows.

Synonyms

Role-Based Prompting, Voice and Tone Prompting, Character Prompting

PBP
