IEC 61850 GOOSE Messaging

A fast, peer-to-peer messaging mechanism in IEC 61850 that broadcasts protection and control events across a substation LAN, enabling millisecond trip, interlock, and status functions without a master polling architecture.

In detail

IEC 61850 GOOSE Messaging is a publish-subscribe communication method used in substation automation to rapidly deliver protection and control events over Ethernet. Instead of using traditional polled protocols, devices publish GOOSE frames that are multicast on the substation LAN. Subscribers filter and process only the messages they are configured to accept, which allows high speed, deterministic responses for functions like breaker trip, interlocking, and status exchange. GOOSE supports redundancy strategies, such as parallel PRP or HSR networks, to meet stringent reliability targets. Engineering of signal datasets, data types, and subscriptions is defined in Substation Configuration Language files, ensuring consistent, vendor-neutral configuration. While focused on substation peer-to-peer needs, GOOSE can be coordinated with higher level systems like DERMS through gateways that translate between IEC 61850 models and other protocols, aligning field execution with system-wide control and standards such as IEEE 1547.

Synonyms

IEC 61850 event messaging, peer-to-peer substation messaging
