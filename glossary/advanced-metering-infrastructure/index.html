Advanced Metering Infrastructure

A networked metering system with smart meters, communications, headend, and data management that enables interval data collection, remote control, outage detection, and integration with demand response and pricing programs.

In detail

Advanced Metering Infrastructure is the end-to-end system that connects smart meters to utility enterprise platforms. It includes field meters, neighborhood or wide-area communications, the AMI headend, and a meter data management system. AMI provides interval reads, remote connect or disconnect, outage and restoration notifications, tamper alerts, and power quality data. It enables time-of-use pricing, demand response, remote firmware updates, and customer usage feedback through portals and APIs. AMI data feeds forecasting, billing, and operational systems such as ADMS and DER platforms. Secure communications and device identity are essential, with support for encryption and authentication across the network. When integrated with IEEE 2030.5 and program platforms, AMI can deliver event signals and validate performance. AMI is a foundational layer for modern grid operations and customer programs, improving reliability, efficiency, and visibility across residential and commercial segments.

Synonyms

Smart metering system, AMI communications network, Metering headend and MDMS

Abbreviations

AMI
