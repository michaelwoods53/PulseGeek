Model Interpretability

The degree to which a person can understand how model inputs influence outputs, supporting trust, debugging, and responsible decision-making.

In detail

Model interpretability is the ability to understand how an AI system produces its outputs. Interpretable models make it easier to find problems, explain decisions to stakeholders, and comply with policies. Techniques include simple, inherently interpretable models and post-hoc methods that explain complex models. Feature importance, partial dependence, and counterfactuals can reveal drivers of predictions. Interpretability has limits and can be misleading if not validated. It should be used with clear documentation, fairness testing, and governance processes to ensure explanations match model behavior and support responsible use.

Synonyms

interpretable ML, transparent modeling
