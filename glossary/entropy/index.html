Entropy

Entropy measures uncertainty in data. Systems need strong entropy to generate keys, seeds, and random numbers that attackers cannot predict or reproduce.

In detail

Entropy quantifies how unpredictable a source is. In cryptography, high-entropy inputs are essential for secure key generation, seeds, and nonces. If randomness is weak or biased, attackers may guess private keys or replay values, breaking security. Blockchains depend on reliable entropy for wallets, signature schemes, and on-chain protocols that need randomness for fair selection. Good designs mix multiple sources, such as system randomness, hardware noise, and verifiable beacons, and they condition outputs through hash functions. Careful testing and monitoring help catch failures early. Protecting entropy sources and avoiding reuse of nonces are key practices for long-term safety.

Synonyms

randomness, unpredictability
