IEEE 2030.5 Smart Energy Profile

An application-layer protocol for secure, IP-based energy device communications, widely used for DER control, customer information, and AMI-driven programs, including profiles like CSIP for utility-scale deployments.

In detail

IEEE 2030.5 Smart Energy Profile defines an IP-based application protocol that enables secure, standardized communication between utilities and end devices such as inverters, gateways, meters, and energy management systems. It supports resource discovery, telemetry, event and price signaling, and control of distributed energy resources and load flexibility programs. IEEE 2030.5 uses web services concepts, supports TLS-based security, and is adaptable to various network transports, including broadband and AMI backhaul. Profiles such as CSIP define constrained subsets and implementation rules to improve interoperability for DER. Utilities adopt IEEE 2030.5 to coordinate grid services like volt-var, frequency-watt, ride-through, and demand response, while integrating with enterprise systems through CIM-aligned data and APIs. Its model-driven approach reduces custom integration, facilitates certification, and enables scalable onboarding of devices from multiple vendors across residential, commercial, and utility-scale contexts.

Synonyms

Smart Energy Profile 2, SEP for DER and AMI, Utility DER communications profile

Abbreviations

IEEE 2030.5, SEP 2.0
