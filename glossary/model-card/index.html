Model Card

A standardized report that documents a model's purpose, data, performance, fairness considerations, limitations, and monitoring plans to support responsible deployment, stakeholder understanding, and ongoing governance.

In detail

A model card is a structured documentation artifact that communicates what a model does, how it was built, and how it should be used. It typically includes the intended purpose and scope, training and evaluation datasets, performance across relevant groups, known limitations and failure modes, ethical and fairness considerations, and monitoring and retraining plans. Clear disclosure of data provenance, preprocessing, and feature selection helps stakeholders assess risks and suitability for their context. Quantitative sections summarize metrics, including fairness and robustness results, while narrative sections outline assumptions, caveats, and usage restrictions. Strong model cards are versioned, linked to dataset documentation, and updated when models change or new evidence arises. They enable informed decision making by product owners, risk teams, and regulators, and promote accountability by making tradeoffs and controls explicit. Model cards complement datasheets for datasets and can note privacy safeguards such as differential privacy and federated learning.
