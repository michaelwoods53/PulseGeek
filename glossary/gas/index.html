Gas

Gas measures the computational work needed to execute transactions and smart contracts. Users pay gas fees to miners or validators to include transactions on-chain.

In detail

Gas is a metering unit used to price computation and storage on blockchains like Ethereum. Each operation in a smart contract consumes a specific amount of gas. Users set a gas limit and price, which determines how much they are willing to spend and how quickly a transaction may be included. Gas fees help prevent spam and ensure network resources are used fairly. Developers optimize gas by reducing storage writes, using efficient data structures, and minimizing heavy computations. Understanding gas costs is key to budgeting, user experience, and contract design.

Synonyms

transaction gas, gas fee
