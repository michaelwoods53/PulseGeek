Data Minimization

A principle and practice of collecting, processing, and retaining only the minimum data necessary for a specific purpose, reducing privacy risk, bias exposure, cost, and regulatory obligations across the AI lifecycle.

In detail

Data minimization is a core privacy and governance principle that instructs teams to limit data collection, use, and retention to what is strictly necessary for a clearly defined purpose. In practice, it means specifying purpose up front, removing unnecessary attributes, aggregating or anonymizing where feasible, setting time-bound retention, and preventing scope creep through access controls and review. Applying minimization curbs attack surface, mitigates bias by reducing sensitive or proxy attributes, and lowers compliance burdens. For AI projects, teams define model objectives, document why each feature is needed, use synthetic or sampled data when appropriate, and periodically revalidate necessity during monitoring. Minimization pairs well with federated learning to avoid centralizing raw data and with differential privacy to further limit disclosure. Documentation artifacts such as datasheets and model cards should describe data selection, justification, and retention policies, creating traceability and accountability for stakeholders and auditors.

Synonyms

purpose limitation, data minimisation, data economy
