Negative Prompting

A technique that specifies what the model should avoid, such as styles, objects, or tones. Common in image generation and text tasks to reduce artifacts, bias, and unwanted content.

In detail

Negative prompting tells a model what not to produce. In text-to-image systems, it commonly excludes unwanted elements like blur, extra limbs, or specific colors. In text generation, it can exclude topics, tones, claims, or sources that are off-limits. Negative prompts act as constraints that reduce artifacts, bias, and policy violations, and they help maintain brand or safety standards. Good practice is to make exclusions concrete and testable, prioritize a short list of critical negatives, and pair them with positive requirements so the model still knows what to do. You can measure impact using evaluation rubrics, automated screens for toxicity or policy terms, and A/B tests. Overly aggressive negatives can degrade quality or push the model into evasive language, so balance is required. Negative prompting often complements persona-based prompts and zero-shot instructions, and fits into broader governance workflows.

Synonyms

Exclusion Prompting, Do-Not-Include Prompts, Negative Constraints

Abbreviations

NP
