Explainability

Explainability clarifies why a model made a decision. It supports trust, debugging, compliance, and better human oversight, especially in high-stakes use cases.

In detail

Explainability refers to techniques and practices that help people understand how and why a model produced a given output. Methods include feature importance, example-based explanations, and surrogate models. Clear explanations increase trust, reveal data issues, and support regulatory requirements. Explainability should be matched to audience needs, from engineers to business users and auditors, and paired with documentation that records assumptions and limits.

Synonyms

model transparency, interpretability
