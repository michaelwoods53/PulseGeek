Bidirectional Search

A search that runs from start and goal simultaneously to meet in the middle.

In detail

Bidirectional methods expand frontiers from both ends, often reducing search depth dramatically. Variants exist for Dijkstra and A*, improving runtime on large maps with a single goal.

Synonyms

two-ended search
