Distributed Energy Resource Management System

A software platform that monitors, forecasts, and controls distributed energy resources at scale, coordinating inverter settings, demand response, and market participation while meeting feeder constraints and grid reliability goals.

In detail

A Distributed Energy Resource Management System is a utility or aggregator platform designed to coordinate large fleets of distributed energy resources, including solar inverters, battery systems, EV chargers, and flexible loads. DERMS integrates telemetry, forecasts, and distribution network constraints to compute safe, effective control actions. It enables functions such as Volt/VAR optimization, frequency support, peak shaving, and load shifting, while honoring interconnection rules and device capabilities defined by standards like IEEE 1547. Through standard protocols and data models, DERMS exchanges information with AMI, ADMS, markets, and field devices. It can manage both direct control and price or signal based programs, and it often includes validation and measurement tools to verify performance. Effective DERMS deployments rely on accurate network models, device registration, and cybersecure communications, and can be engineered to interoperate with IEC 61850 based substation systems via gateways.

Synonyms

DER management platform, fleet DER control system

Abbreviations

DERMS
