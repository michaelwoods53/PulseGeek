Glossary

A–Z glossary of key terms used on PulseGeek.

ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVWXYZ

A

AI Governance Framework
A structured set of policies, roles, and processes that guide how AI is designed, deployed, and monitored to meet ethical and compliance goals.
Algorithmic Bias
Systematic errors in AI outputs that unfairly favor or disadvantage groups or individuals due to data issues, model design, or deployment context.

C

Chain-of-Thought Prompting
A prompting pattern that encourages models to explain steps or reasoning before giving a final answer, improving accuracy on complex or multi-step problems.

D

Demographic Parity
A fairness criterion that aims for equal positive outcome rates across groups, regardless of true labels or ground truth distributions.

E

Equalized Odds
A fairness criterion requiring similar true positive and false positive rates across groups to balance errors and reduce unequal harms.
Explainable AI
Methods and tools that create understandable explanations for AI decisions, helping teams communicate, debug, and meet accountability and compliance needs.

F

Fairness Metric
A quantitative measure used to assess whether an AI model treats groups fairly, often comparing error rates, selection rates, or outcomes across protected groups.
Few-Shot Prompting
A technique that includes a few labeled examples in the prompt so the model can learn the task format and style, improving consistency and accuracy.

G

Guardrails
Rules, prompts, and checks that prevent unsafe, off-policy, or low-quality outputs, helping teams keep AI behavior compliant and consistent.

L

LIME (Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations)
An explanation approach that fits simple local models around individual predictions to show which features influenced the outcome.

M

Model Interpretability
The degree to which a person can understand how model inputs influence outputs, supporting trust, debugging, and responsible decision-making.

P

Prompt Chaining
A workflow where outputs from one prompt feed into the next, breaking complex tasks into smaller steps for better control and higher quality.
Prompt Engineering
The practice of designing inputs that guide AI models to produce accurate, useful, and safe outputs for a task, using patterns, examples, constraints, and evaluation to improve results.
Prompt Template
A reusable structure for prompts with placeholders for variables, examples, and constraints, designed to produce consistent outputs across similar tasks.

R

Retrieval-Augmented Generation
A technique that fetches relevant documents and adds them to the prompt, helping the model generate grounded, up-to-date, and verifiable responses.

S

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)
A model-agnostic method that attributes a prediction to each feature using game theory, offering consistent and locally accurate explanations.
System Prompt
A high-priority instruction that sets role, tone, and boundaries for the model, shaping behavior across an entire conversation or workflow.
