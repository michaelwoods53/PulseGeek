Glossary

A–Z glossary of key terms used on PulseGeek.

ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVWXYZ

A

A/B Testing for Prompts
A controlled experiment that compares two or more prompt variants on defined metrics. It helps select better prompts, estimate impact, and reduce risk before broad deployment.
Advanced Distribution Management System
A utility operations platform that integrates SCADA, DMS, and OMS to monitor and control distribution grids, optimize switching and voltage, and coordinate DER, AMI data, and customer programs in real time.
Advanced Metering Infrastructure
A networked metering system with smart meters, communications, headend, and data management that enables interval data collection, remote control, outage detection, and integration with demand response and pricing programs.
AI Governance Framework
A structured set of policies, roles, and processes that guide how AI is designed, deployed, and monitored to meet ethical and compliance goals.
Algorithmic Bias
Systematic errors in AI outputs that unfairly favor or disadvantage groups or individuals due to data issues, model design, or deployment context.

C

Chain-of-Thought Prompting
A prompting pattern that encourages models to explain steps or reasoning before giving a final answer, improving accuracy on complex or multi-step problems.
Common Grid Model Exchange Specification
A set of IEC CIM profiles used to exchange consistent transmission and distribution network models across organizations, supporting planning, operations, and market processes with standardized semantics and validation rules.

D

Data Minimization
A principle and practice of collecting, processing, and retaining only the minimum data necessary for a specific purpose, reducing privacy risk, bias exposure, cost, and regulatory obligations across the AI lifecycle.
Datasheet for Datasets
A structured documentation template that records a dataset's origin, collection process, consent, composition, quality issues, and intended uses to improve transparency, fairness, and risk governance throughout the AI lifecycle.
Demographic Parity
A fairness criterion that aims for equal positive outcome rates across groups, regardless of true labels or ground truth distributions.
Differential Privacy
A mathematical framework that adds calibrated noise to data or queries to protect individual privacy while preserving useful aggregate insights, governed by a tunable privacy budget that quantifies disclosure risk.
Distributed Energy Resource Management System
A software platform that monitors, forecasts, and controls distributed energy resources at scale, coordinating inverter settings, demand response, and market participation while meeting feeder constraints and grid reliability goals.

E

Equalized Odds
A fairness criterion requiring similar true positive and false positive rates across groups to balance errors and reduce unequal harms.
Explainable AI
Methods and tools that create understandable explanations for AI decisions, helping teams communicate, debug, and meet accountability and compliance needs.

F

Fairness Metric
A quantitative measure used to assess whether an AI model treats groups fairly, often comparing error rates, selection rates, or outcomes across protected groups.
Federated Learning
A training approach that keeps raw data on devices or silos and sends only model updates to a server for aggregation, improving privacy, compliance, and scalability while reducing centralization risk.
Few-Shot Prompting
A technique that includes a few labeled examples in the prompt so the model can learn the task format and style, improving consistency and accuracy.

G

Guardrails
Rules, prompts, and checks that prevent unsafe, off-policy, or low-quality outputs, helping teams keep AI behavior compliant and consistent.

I

IEC 61850 GOOSE Messaging
A fast, peer-to-peer messaging mechanism in IEC 61850 that broadcasts protection and control events across a substation LAN, enabling millisecond trip, interlock, and status functions without a master polling architecture.
IEC Common Information Model
A standardized power system data model used by utilities to represent assets, networks, and operations, enabling consistent data exchange and integration across EMS, DMS, DER, and planning tools.
IEEE 1547 Interconnection Standard
A core standard that defines technical requirements for interconnecting distributed energy resources with the electric grid, including voltage and frequency ride-through, Volt/VAR, frequency-watt, interoperability, and testing.
IEEE 2030.5 Smart Energy Profile
An application-layer protocol for secure, IP-based energy device communications, widely used for DER control, customer information, and AMI-driven programs, including profiles like CSIP for utility-scale deployments.
ISO 15118 Vehicle-to-Grid Communication Interface
A standard for secure digital communication between EVs and charging stations, enabling Plug and Charge, contract handling, smart charging, and V2G energy services with robust authentication and certificate management.

L

LIME (Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations)
An explanation approach that fits simple local models around individual predictions to show which features influenced the outcome.

M

Model Card
A standardized report that documents a model's purpose, data, performance, fairness considerations, limitations, and monitoring plans to support responsible deployment, stakeholder understanding, and ongoing governance.
Model Interpretability
The degree to which a person can understand how model inputs influence outputs, supporting trust, debugging, and responsible decision-making.

N

Negative Prompting
A technique that specifies what the model should avoid, such as styles, objects, or tones. Common in image generation and text tasks to reduce artifacts, bias, and unwanted content.

O

Open Charge Point Protocol
An open protocol that manages communication between EV charging stations and central systems, enabling remote control, monitoring, billing, smart charging, and interoperability across multi-vendor charging networks.

P

Persona-Based Prompting
A strategy that assigns the model a role, audience, and tone to shape style and decisions. It improves consistency for marketing, support, and teaching tasks across channels and formats.
Prompt Chaining
A workflow where outputs from one prompt feed into the next, breaking complex tasks into smaller steps for better control and higher quality.
Prompt Engineering
The practice of designing inputs that guide AI models to produce accurate, useful, and safe outputs for a task, using patterns, examples, constraints, and evaluation to improve results.
Prompt Evaluation Rubric
A structured scoring guide that rates prompt outputs against criteria like accuracy, completeness, style, and safety. Used to compare prompts, track changes, and guide iterative improvement.
Prompt Leakage
An information security risk where hidden instructions, system prompts, or proprietary context become exposed through outputs or attack prompts. It can harm safety, privacy, and competitive advantage.
Prompt Template
A reusable structure for prompts with placeholders for variables, examples, and constraints, designed to produce consistent outputs across similar tasks.

R

Retrieval-Augmented Generation
A technique that fetches relevant documents and adds them to the prompt, helping the model generate grounded, up-to-date, and verifiable responses.

S

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)
A model-agnostic method that attributes a prediction to each feature using game theory, offering consistent and locally accurate explanations.
Substation Configuration Language
An XML-based language in IEC 61850 for describing substation systems, IED capabilities, and communications. SCL files enable consistent engineering, interoperability, and testing across multi-vendor protection and control deployments.
System Prompt
A high-priority instruction that sets role, tone, and boundaries for the model, shaping behavior across an entire conversation or workflow.

V

Volt/VAR Control
A control strategy that manages voltage and reactive power on distribution feeders using inverter functions, capacitor banks, regulators, and setpoint curves to improve power quality, reduce losses, and support hosting capacity.

Z

Zero-Shot Prompting
A prompting technique where the model is asked to perform a task without examples. Clear instructions, constraints, and role hints guide behavior when training data or examples are unavailable.
