Glossary

A–Z glossary of key terms used on PulseGeek.

ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVWXYZ

A

A/B Testing for Prompts
A controlled experiment that compares two or more prompt variants on defined metrics. It helps select better prompts, estimate impact, and reduce risk before broad deployment.
Academic Integrity
Academic integrity means being honest in schoolwork, giving credit, and following rules. It includes responsible use of AI tools and proper citation.
Accessibility in Education
Accessibility ensures learning materials and tools work for all learners, including those with disabilities, using features like captions, alt text, and screen reader support.
Adaptive Learning
Adaptive learning systems change practice and difficulty in real time based on student performance data, offering targeted support to improve outcomes.
Advanced Distribution Management System
A utility operations platform that integrates SCADA, DMS, and OMS to monitor and control distribution grids, optimize switching and voltage, and coordinate DER, AMI data, and customer programs in real time.
Advanced Metering Infrastructure
A networked metering system with smart meters, communications, headend, and data management that enables interval data collection, remote control, outage detection, and integration with demand response and pricing programs.
AI Ethics
AI ethics guides how AI should be designed and used to be fair, transparent, and safe, with respect for people’s rights and well-being.
AI Governance Framework
A structured set of policies, roles, and processes that guide how AI is designed, deployed, and monitored to meet ethical and compliance goals.
AI Homework Help
AI homework help tools give hints, explanations, and examples to guide students, ideally supporting learning without doing the work for them.
AI Virtual Assistant
An AI virtual assistant helps users complete tasks like scheduling, reminders, and quick answers, and can aid teachers with planning and grading support.
Algorithmic Bias
Systematic errors in AI outputs that unfairly favor or disadvantage groups or individuals due to data issues, model design, or deployment context.
Artificial Intelligence
Artificial intelligence is the field of building computer systems that can perform tasks that usually require human thinking, such as understanding language, recognizing patterns, and making decisions.
Assessment Analytics
Assessment analytics uses data from quizzes, assignments, and behavior to understand progress and guide instruction, often with help from AI tools.

B

Blockchain Explorer
A blockchain explorer is a web tool to search blocks, transactions, addresses, and contract events, helping users verify activity and debug issues.
Blockchain Security
Blockchain security covers protecting networks, nodes, and smart contracts from attacks, bugs, and misconfigurations, using audits, testing, and strong design patterns.

C

Chain-of-Thought Prompting
A prompting pattern that encourages models to explain steps or reasoning before giving a final answer, improving accuracy on complex or multi-step problems.
Chatbot
A chatbot is a software agent that interacts through text or voice to answer questions, guide tasks, or provide tutoring and support.
Classroom AI Policy
A classroom AI policy explains how students and teachers may use AI tools, including allowed uses, boundaries, and responsibilities.
Cloud AI
Cloud AI delivers AI features through online services, allowing schools to use powerful models without managing complex infrastructure.
Common Grid Model Exchange Specification
A set of IEC CIM profiles used to exchange consistent transmission and distribution network models across organizations, supporting planning, operations, and market processes with standardized semantics and validation rules.
Conversational AI
Conversational AI combines language understanding and generation to hold useful, natural conversations with users across chat or voice channels.
Curriculum Alignment
Curriculum alignment ensures activities, content, and assessments match learning standards and goals, even when AI tools suggest or generate materials.

D

Data Minimization
A principle and practice of collecting, processing, and retaining only the minimum data necessary for a specific purpose, reducing privacy risk, bias exposure, cost, and regulatory obligations across the AI lifecycle.
Data Privacy
Data privacy covers how personal information is collected, stored, and used. Schools must protect student data when adopting AI tools and services.
Datasheet for Datasets
A structured documentation template that records a dataset's origin, collection process, consent, composition, quality issues, and intended uses to improve transparency, fairness, and risk governance throughout the AI lifecycle.
Decentralized Application (DApp)
A DApp is an application that runs on a blockchain, using smart contracts for core logic and often a web or mobile interface for users.
Demographic Parity
A fairness criterion that aims for equal positive outcome rates across groups, regardless of true labels or ground truth distributions.
Differential Privacy
A mathematical framework that adds calibrated noise to data or queries to protect individual privacy while preserving useful aggregate insights, governed by a tunable privacy budget that quantifies disclosure risk.
Digital Signature
A digital signature proves a message came from a private key holder and was not changed. It provides authenticity and integrity, which blockchains use to authorize transactions.
Digital Twin
A digital twin is a virtual model of a real system used to simulate, test, and learn from different scenarios and data inputs.
Distributed Energy Resource Management System
A software platform that monitors, forecasts, and controls distributed energy resources at scale, coordinating inverter settings, demand response, and market participation while meeting feeder constraints and grid reliability goals.
Distributed Ledger
A distributed ledger is a shared database replicated across many nodes, where entries are agreed upon and difficult to change without consensus.

E

EdTech
EdTech covers tools and platforms used in teaching and learning, including AI-powered applications for content, assessment, and classroom management.
Entropy
Entropy measures uncertainty in data. Systems need strong entropy to generate keys, seeds, and random numbers that attackers cannot predict or reproduce.
Equalized Odds
A fairness criterion requiring similar true positive and false positive rates across groups to balance errors and reduce unequal harms.
Ethereum Virtual Machine
The EVM is the runtime that executes smart contract bytecode on Ethereum and compatible chains. It defines how code runs, uses gas, and accesses blockchain state.
Explainable AI
Methods and tools that create understandable explanations for AI decisions, helping teams communicate, debug, and meet accountability and compliance needs.

F

Fairness Metric
A quantitative measure used to assess whether an AI model treats groups fairly, often comparing error rates, selection rates, or outcomes across protected groups.
Federated Learning
A training approach that keeps raw data on devices or silos and sends only model updates to a server for aggregation, improving privacy, compliance, and scalability while reducing centralization risk.
Few-Shot Prompting
A technique that includes a few labeled examples in the prompt so the model can learn the task format and style, improving consistency and accuracy.
Formal Verification
Formal verification uses math to prove that smart contract code meets a precise specification. It helps catch critical bugs and logic errors before deployment.

G

Gas
Gas measures the computational work needed to execute transactions and smart contracts. Users pay gas fees to miners or validators to include transactions on-chain.
Generative AI
Generative AI creates new content like text, images, or code based on patterns learned from training data. It can assist with drafting, feedback, and creative tasks.
Guardrails
Rules, prompts, and checks that prevent unsafe, off-policy, or low-quality outputs, helping teams keep AI behavior compliant and consistent.

H

Hash Function
A hash function maps any input to a fixed-length output, called a digest. Good hash functions are one-way, collision-resistant, and spread outputs evenly to detect tampering in data or blocks.

I

IEC 61850 GOOSE Messaging
A fast, peer-to-peer messaging mechanism in IEC 61850 that broadcasts protection and control events across a substation LAN, enabling millisecond trip, interlock, and status functions without a master polling architecture.
IEC Common Information Model
A standardized power system data model used by utilities to represent assets, networks, and operations, enabling consistent data exchange and integration across EMS, DMS, DER, and planning tools.
IEEE 1547 Interconnection Standard
A core standard that defines technical requirements for interconnecting distributed energy resources with the electric grid, including voltage and frequency ride-through, Volt/VAR, frequency-watt, interoperability, and testing.
IEEE 2030.5 Smart Energy Profile
An application-layer protocol for secure, IP-based energy device communications, widely used for DER control, customer information, and AMI-driven programs, including profiles like CSIP for utility-scale deployments.
ISO 15118 Vehicle-to-Grid Communication Interface
A standard for secure digital communication between EVs and charging stations, enabling Plug and Charge, contract handling, smart charging, and V2G energy services with robust authentication and certificate management.

L

Learning Management System
A learning management system hosts courses, assignments, and communication. It can integrate AI features like analytics, recommendations, and automated feedback.
LIME (Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations)
An explanation approach that fits simple local models around individual predictions to show which features influenced the outcome.

M

Machine Learning
Machine learning is a set of methods that let computers learn patterns from data and improve at tasks without being explicitly programmed for every rule.
Mempool
The mempool holds unconfirmed transactions waiting to be added to a block. It affects ordering, fees, and attack surfaces like front-running and MEV.
Merkle Tree
A Merkle tree is a tree of hashes that summarizes many data items with a single root. It enables quick proofs that specific items are included without downloading all data.
Model Card
A standardized report that documents a model's purpose, data, performance, fairness considerations, limitations, and monitoring plans to support responsible deployment, stakeholder understanding, and ongoing governance.
Model Interpretability
The degree to which a person can understand how model inputs influence outputs, supporting trust, debugging, and responsible decision-making.

N

Negative Prompting
A technique that specifies what the model should avoid, such as styles, objects, or tones. Common in image generation and text tasks to reduce artifacts, bias, and unwanted content.

O

Open Charge Point Protocol
An open protocol that manages communication between EV charging stations and central systems, enabling remote control, monitoring, billing, smart charging, and interoperability across multi-vendor charging networks.
Oracle
An oracle supplies off-chain data to on-chain contracts, such as prices or weather. Secure oracle design prevents manipulation and minimizes trust in single data sources.

P

Patricia Trie
A Patricia trie is a compact key-value tree that uses shared prefixes to save space. With hashing, it provides verifiable state roots for fast lookups and proofs.
Persona-Based Prompting
A strategy that assigns the model a role, audience, and tone to shape style and decisions. It improves consistency for marketing, support, and teaching tasks across channels and formats.
Personalized Learning
Personalized learning adapts content, pace, and support to each student’s needs and goals, often using data and AI-driven recommendations.
Prompt Chaining
A workflow where outputs from one prompt feed into the next, breaking complex tasks into smaller steps for better control and higher quality.
Prompt Engineering
The practice of designing inputs that guide AI models to produce accurate, useful, and safe outputs for a task, using patterns, examples, constraints, and evaluation to improve results.
Prompt Evaluation Rubric
A structured scoring guide that rates prompt outputs against criteria like accuracy, completeness, style, and safety. Used to compare prompts, track changes, and guide iterative improvement.
Prompt Leakage
An information security risk where hidden instructions, system prompts, or proprietary context become exposed through outputs or attack prompts. It can harm safety, privacy, and competitive advantage.
Prompt Template
A reusable structure for prompts with placeholders for variables, examples, and constraints, designed to produce consistent outputs across similar tasks.
Public Key Cryptography
Public key cryptography uses a key pair: a public key for verification or encryption and a private key for signing or decryption. It enables secure identity and communication in open networks.

R

Reentrancy
Reentrancy is a vulnerability where a contract’s external call lets another contract call back before state updates finish, enabling repeated withdrawals or logic misuse.
Responsible AI
Responsible AI means building and using AI systems that are safe, fair, transparent, and aligned with human values, with checks and accountability.
Retrieval-Augmented Generation
A technique that fetches relevant documents and adds them to the prompt, helping the model generate grounded, up-to-date, and verifiable responses.

S

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)
A model-agnostic method that attributes a prediction to each feature using game theory, offering consistent and locally accurate explanations.
Smart Contract
A smart contract is code on a blockchain that runs automatically when rules are met. It enforces agreements without intermediaries, using transparent, tamper-resistant logic.
Solidity
Solidity is a programming language for writing smart contracts, mainly on EVM-compatible blockchains. It looks like JavaScript and adds features for blockchain data, events, and access control.
Substation Configuration Language
An XML-based language in IEC 61850 for describing substation systems, IED capabilities, and communications. SCL files enable consistent engineering, interoperability, and testing across multi-vendor protection and control deployments.
System Prompt
A high-priority instruction that sets role, tone, and boundaries for the model, shaping behavior across an entire conversation or workflow.

T

Teacher Workload Automation
Teacher workload automation uses AI to draft materials, organize tasks, and support grading, helping teachers focus more on instruction and feedback.

U

Upgradeable Proxy
An upgradeable proxy lets a contract’s logic be replaced while keeping the same address and state, enabling fixes and improvements after deployment.

V

Verifiable Random Function
A verifiable random function produces randomness tied to a public key, along with a proof that anyone can verify. It supports fair leader election and lotteries without trusted parties.
Volt/VAR Control
A control strategy that manages voltage and reactive power on distribution feeders using inverter functions, capacitor banks, regulators, and setpoint curves to improve power quality, reduce losses, and support hosting capacity.

Z

Zero-Knowledge Proof
A zero-knowledge proof lets someone convince a verifier that a statement is true without revealing the underlying information. It enables privacy and compression in blockchain systems.
Zero-Shot Prompting
A prompting technique where the model is asked to perform a task without examples. Clear instructions, constraints, and role hints guide behavior when training data or examples are unavailable.
