Glossary

A–Z glossary of key terms used on PulseGeek.

ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVWXYZ

A

A/B Testing
Comparing two versions to see which performs better.
A/B Testing for Prompts
A controlled experiment that compares two or more prompt variants on defined metrics. It helps select better prompts, estimate impact, and reduce risk before broad deployment.
Academic Integrity
Academic integrity means being honest in schoolwork, giving credit, and following rules. It includes responsible use of AI tools and proper citation.
Accessibility in Education
Accessibility ensures learning materials and tools work for all learners, including those with disabilities, using features like captions, alt text, and screen reader support.
Accounts Payable Automation
Using AI and workflows to capture invoices, match POs, route approvals, and pay vendors faster with fewer errors.
Adaptive Learning
Adaptive learning systems change practice and difficulty in real time based on student performance data, offering targeted support to improve outcomes.
Advanced Distribution Management System
A utility operations platform that integrates SCADA, DMS, and OMS to monitor and control distribution grids, optimize switching and voltage, and coordinate DER, AMI data, and customer programs in real time.
Advanced Metering Infrastructure
A networked metering system with smart meters, communications, headend, and data management that enables interval data collection, remote control, outage detection, and integration with demand response and pricing programs.
AI Adoption Roadmap
A phased plan to pilot, scale, and govern AI in the business.
AI Bots
Software agents that automate steps or answer questions. In retail they handle routine requests, freeing teams to solve complex problems.
AI Data Analytics
Using AI to analyze large datasets and produce actionable insights. It powers demand planning, pricing, and store optimization.
AI Ethics
AI ethics guides how AI should be designed and used to be fair, transparent, and safe, with respect for people’s rights and well-being.
AI Governance Framework
A structured set of policies, roles, and processes that guide how AI is designed, deployed, and monitored to meet ethical and compliance goals.
AI Homework Help
AI homework help tools give hints, explanations, and examples to guide students, ideally supporting learning without doing the work for them.
AI Operating Model
The structure and processes for running ai at scale.
AI Readiness
AI readiness measures how prepared an organization is to adopt AI, including strategy, data quality, talent, technology, and governance foundations.
AI Strategy
A plan to align AI initiatives with business goals.
AI Virtual Assistant
An AI virtual assistant helps users complete tasks like scheduling, reminders, and quick answers, and can aid teachers with planning and grading support.
Algorithmic Bias
Systematic errors in AI outputs that unfairly favor or disadvantage groups or individuals due to data issues, model design, or deployment context.
Alternative Data
Non-traditional datasets used in investment analysis.
AML
Regulations to prevent money laundering and terrorist financing.
Analytics Pipeline
The steps to collect, clean, and prepare data for analysis and AI.
Anomaly Detection
Techniques that find unusual data points or patterns, like odd expenses or duplicate payments, which may signal errors, policy breaks, or fraud risks in finance processes.
Anti-Money Laundering
Policies and analytics to detect and report suspicious activity that hides illegal funds, often using risk scores, alerts, and case reviews.
API
A set of rules for connecting software systems.
Artificial Intelligence
Artificial intelligence is the field of building computer systems that can perform tasks that usually require human thinking, such as understanding language, recognizing patterns, and making decisions.
Assessment Analytics
Assessment analytics uses data from quizzes, assignments, and behavior to understand progress and guide instruction, often with help from AI tools.
Assortment Optimization
Choosing the right mix of products for each store or channel to meet demand and improve margins. AI analyzes sales, basket patterns, and space limits to recommend the best lineup.
Audit Trail
A detailed record of actions and changes, showing who did what and when, so reviews and compliance checks are possible.
Autoencoder
Neural network that learns compressed representations.

B

Backtesting
Testing a model or strategy on historical data.
Basket Analysis
Finding products that are often bought together to improve assortments, cross-sells, and promotions. AI reveals patterns across stores and shopper segments.
Blockchain Explorer
A blockchain explorer is a web tool to search blocks, transactions, addresses, and contract events, helping users verify activity and debug issues.
Blockchain Security
Blockchain security covers protecting networks, nodes, and smart contracts from attacks, bugs, and misconfigurations, using audits, testing, and strong design patterns.
Business Case
A structured argument that explains why a project should be funded.
Business Intelligence
Tools and processes for turning data into insights and dashboards.

C

Capital Markets
Markets for raising and trading capital.
Cash Flow Forecasting
Predicting cash inflows and outflows over time.
Center of Excellence
A center of excellence is a team that defines standards, shares best practices, and accelerates AI delivery across the organization.
Chain-of-Thought Prompting
A prompting pattern that encourages models to explain steps or reasoning before giving a final answer, improving accuracy on complex or multi-step problems.
Change Management
Change management helps people adopt new ways of working. For AI, it aligns roles, training, communication, and incentives to support safe, lasting adoption.
Chatbot
A chatbot is a software agent that interacts through text or voice to answer questions, guide tasks, or provide tutoring and support.
Churn Prediction
Estimating which customers are likely to leave.
Class Imbalance
When one class has far fewer samples than others.
Classification
A model that assigns items to predefined labels.
Classification Model
A model that assigns inputs to discrete categories.
Classroom AI Policy
A classroom AI policy explains how students and teachers may use AI tools, including allowed uses, boundaries, and responsibilities.
Cloud AI
Cloud AI delivers AI features through online services, allowing schools to use powerful models without managing complex infrastructure.
Clustering
Unsupervised grouping of similar data points.
Common Grid Model Exchange Specification
A set of IEC CIM profiles used to exchange consistent transmission and distribution network models across organizations, supporting planning, operations, and market processes with standardized semantics and validation rules.
Computer Vision
AI that understands images and video. In retail it tracks shelves, shoppers, and queues to improve availability, safety, and service.
Computer Vision Checkout
Checkout that uses cameras and sensors to auto-charge.
Computer Vision Heatmap
Heatmaps generated from camera analytics.
Confidence Interval
A range around a forecast that shows the uncertainty of predictions, helping plan for best and worst cases.
Confusion Matrix
A table that shows correct and incorrect predictions, helping teams understand false positives and false negatives in models.
Contact Center Automation
Using AI to streamline support interactions.
Control Framework
Policies and procedures that help keep financial data accurate, secure, and compliant when AI is used in processes.
Conversational AI
Conversational AI combines language understanding and generation to hold useful, natural conversations with users across chat or voice channels.
Convolutional Neural Network
Neural network using convolutions for pattern learning.
Cost Benefit Analysis
A method to compare the costs and benefits of a project.
Cost of Ownership
The full cost to run and maintain an AI solution over time.
Cross-Validation
A method to estimate model performance reliably.
Curriculum Alignment
Curriculum alignment ensures activities, content, and assessments match learning standards and goals, even when AI tools suggest or generate materials.
Customer Experience
How customers perceive interactions across their journey.

D

Data Drift
Changes in the input data distribution that can reduce model quality, such as new vendors, pricing, or formats in finance systems.
Data Governance
Data governance sets policies and roles to manage data quality, access, privacy, and lifecycle, ensuring data is usable, compliant, and secure for AI projects.
Data Lake
A centralized store for raw data from many systems. It feeds AI models for forecasting, pricing, and store analytics.
Data Leakage
When training data contains information from the future.
Data Lineage
Tracking data origins, changes, and usage.
Data Minimization
A principle and practice of collecting, processing, and retaining only the minimum data necessary for a specific purpose, reducing privacy risk, bias exposure, cost, and regulatory obligations across the AI lifecycle.
Data Pipeline
Systems that move, clean, and prepare data from sources into models and dashboards on a reliable schedule.
Data Privacy
Data privacy covers how personal information is collected, stored, and used. Schools must protect student data when adopting AI tools and services.
Data Quality
Data quality measures how fit data is for use. It covers completeness, accuracy, consistency, timeliness, and uniqueness to support strong AI outcomes.
Data Readiness
How fit your data is for AI and analytics tasks.
Data Warehouse
A central store for structured analytics data.
Datasheet for Datasets
A structured documentation template that records a dataset's origin, collection process, consent, composition, quality issues, and intended uses to improve transparency, fairness, and risk governance throughout the AI lifecycle.
Decentralized Application (DApp)
A DApp is an application that runs on a blockchain, using smart contracts for core logic and often a web or mobile interface for users.
Deep Learning
Complex neural models that learn layered representations.
Demand Forecasting
Estimating future customer demand using historical data, trends, and external factors. AI improves accuracy by finding patterns, seasonality, and events that traditional methods often miss.
Demand Sensing
Capturing short-term demand signals from POS, web traffic, and events to adjust forecasts quickly. AI improves responsiveness and reduces stockouts.
Demographic Parity
A fairness criterion that aims for equal positive outcome rates across groups, regardless of true labels or ground truth distributions.
Differential Privacy
A mathematical framework that adds calibrated noise to data or queries to protect individual privacy while preserving useful aggregate insights, governed by a tunable privacy budget that quantifies disclosure risk.
Digital Signature
A digital signature proves a message came from a private key holder and was not changed. It provides authenticity and integrity, which blockchains use to authorize transactions.
Digital Twin
A digital twin is a virtual model of a real system used to simulate, test, and learn from different scenarios and data inputs.
Distributed Energy Resource Management System
A software platform that monitors, forecasts, and controls distributed energy resources at scale, coordinating inverter settings, demand response, and market participation while meeting feeder constraints and grid reliability goals.
Distributed Ledger
A distributed ledger is a shared database replicated across many nodes, where entries are agreed upon and difficult to change without consensus.
Document Intelligence
AI systems that read and structure data from documents.
Document Processing
AI tools that read and extract data from invoices, receipts, and statements so finance teams can automate entry and reduce manual errors.
Dynamic Analysis
Running software in a controlled environment to observe behavior. It captures API calls, network traffic, and artifacts for detection.
Dynamic Pricing
Adjusting prices based on demand, competition, and costs. AI finds the best price at item-store level while meeting margin and inventory goals.

E

Edge AI
Running AI models on local devices instead of the cloud.
Edge Inference
Running AI models on devices close to where data is created.
EdTech
EdTech covers tools and platforms used in teaching and learning, including AI-powered applications for content, assessment, and classroom management.
Embedding
Numeric vectors representing text, code, or binaries.
Embeddings
Numeric vectors that capture meaning or behavior of items like words, APIs, or opcodes. They help models generalize and compare similar items.
Entity Extraction
AI that identifies key fields like vendor names, invoice numbers, dates, and amounts from documents to enable automation.
Entity Linking
The process of connecting different mentions of the same real-world entity across sources. It improves threat intelligence by merging duplicate indicators and names.
Entity Recognition
Finding and labeling key items in text like names or dates.
Entity Resolution
Merging records that refer to the same real-world entity.
Entropy
Entropy measures uncertainty in data. Systems need strong entropy to generate keys, seeds, and random numbers that attackers cannot predict or reproduce.
Equalized Odds
A fairness criterion requiring similar true positive and false positive rates across groups to balance errors and reduce unequal harms.
Ethereum Virtual Machine
The EVM is the runtime that executes smart contract bytecode on Ethereum and compatible chains. It defines how code runs, uses gas, and accesses blockchain state.
Ethical AI
Principles and practices that reduce bias, protect privacy, and make AI more fair and transparent.
ETL
Extract, Transform, Load processes that move and shape data.
ETL and ELT
Processes that move and transform data for analytics and AI.
Explainability
Explainability clarifies why a model made a decision. It supports trust, debugging, compliance, and better human oversight, especially in high-stakes use cases.
Explainable AI
Methods and tools that create understandable explanations for AI decisions, helping teams communicate, debug, and meet accountability and compliance needs.

F

F1 Score
A single measure combining precision and recall.
Fairness Metric
A quantitative measure used to assess whether an AI model treats groups fairly, often comparing error rates, selection rates, or outcomes across protected groups.
False Negative
A malicious event that the model fails to detect. Too many false negatives leave threats unchecked and increase risk.
False Positive
An alert flagged as malicious that is actually benign. High false positive rates waste analyst time and reduce trust in detection systems.
False Positive Rate
Share of benign events incorrectly flagged as threats.
Feature Engineering
Feature engineering transforms raw data into useful inputs for models. It improves signal and model performance through selection, encoding, and aggregation.
Feature Store
A system to manage and serve machine learning features.
Federated Learning
A training approach that keeps raw data on devices or silos and sends only model updates to a server for aggregation, improving privacy, compliance, and scalability while reducing centralization risk.
Few-Shot Prompting
A technique that includes a few labeled examples in the prompt so the model can learn the task format and style, improving consistency and accuracy.
Financial Controls
Policies and checks that protect financial accuracy.
Financial Forecasting
Estimating future financial outcomes using historical data and assumptions.
Foot Traffic
The count of shoppers entering or moving through a store.
Forecast Accuracy
Measures like MAPE or MAE that show how close forecasts are to actuals, guiding model and business decisions.
Formal Verification
Formal verification uses math to prove that smart contract code meets a precise specification. It helps catch critical bugs and logic errors before deployment.
FP&A
A function that plans budgets, forecasts, and performance reporting.
Fraud Detection
The use of data and models to spot and stop suspicious transactions, chargebacks, and identity abuse before losses occur.

G

Gas
Gas measures the computational work needed to execute transactions and smart contracts. Users pay gas fees to miners or validators to include transactions on-chain.
Generative AI
Generative AI creates new content like text, images, or code based on patterns learned from training data. It can assist with drafting, feedback, and creative tasks.
Governance
Policies and roles that guide how AI is built, used, and monitored to stay safe, fair, and compliant.
Guardrails
Rules, prompts, and checks that prevent unsafe, off-policy, or low-quality outputs, helping teams keep AI behavior compliant and consistent.

H

Hash Function
A hash function maps any input to a fixed-length output, called a digest. Good hash functions are one-way, collision-resistant, and spread outputs evenly to detect tampering in data or blocks.
Heatmap
A visual map showing where shoppers spend time or move in a store. AI uses sensors and vision to reveal hot and cold zones for layout and staffing decisions.

I

IEC 61850 GOOSE Messaging
A fast, peer-to-peer messaging mechanism in IEC 61850 that broadcasts protection and control events across a substation LAN, enabling millisecond trip, interlock, and status functions without a master polling architecture.
IEC Common Information Model
A standardized power system data model used by utilities to represent assets, networks, and operations, enabling consistent data exchange and integration across EMS, DMS, DER, and planning tools.
IEEE 1547 Interconnection Standard
A core standard that defines technical requirements for interconnecting distributed energy resources with the electric grid, including voltage and frequency ride-through, Volt/VAR, frequency-watt, interoperability, and testing.
IEEE 2030.5 Smart Energy Profile
An application-layer protocol for secure, IP-based energy device communications, widely used for DER control, customer information, and AMI-driven programs, including profiles like CSIP for utility-scale deployments.
In-Store Analytics
Measuring shopper behavior and store operations using sensors, vision, and sales data. AI turns raw signals into insights for layout, staffing, and merchandising.
Indicator of Compromise
Data points like IPs, domains, hashes, or file paths that signal possible malicious activity. AI helps enrich, group, and score IOCs for better triage.
Intrusion Detection System
A security tool that monitors network or host activity to spot malicious behavior. It uses rules, heuristics, or machine learning to flag suspicious events for review or automated response.
Inventory Optimization
Balancing inventory to avoid stockouts and excess. AI sets targets by item and store using demand forecasts, lead times, and service goals.
IOC Clustering
Grouping similar indicators of compromise to reveal campaigns or shared infrastructure. It helps analysts focus on meaningful patterns instead of single data points.
ISO 15118 Vehicle-to-Grid Communication Interface
A standard for secure digital communication between EVs and charging stations, enabling Plug and Charge, contract handling, smart charging, and V2G energy services with robust authentication and certificate management.

K

Key Performance Indicator
A measurable value that shows how well a goal is being achieved.
Know Your Customer
Processes to verify identity and assess customer risk, often used with AML programs and onboarding controls.
Knowledge Graph
A knowledge graph organizes entities and relationships in a connected structure, improving search, reasoning, and data integration for AI applications.
KPI
A metric that tracks progress toward a goal.
KPI Dashboard
A visual board that tracks key metrics.
KYC
Processes to verify customer identity and risk.

L

Large Language Model
A generative model trained to predict and produce human-like text.
Lead Scoring
Ranking prospects by their likelihood to convert.
Learning Management System
A learning management system hosts courses, assignments, and communication. It can integrate AI features like analytics, recommendations, and automated feedback.
LIME (Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations)
An explanation approach that fits simple local models around individual predictions to show which features influenced the outcome.
Loss Prevention
Methods to reduce theft and inventory shrink.

M

Machine Learning
Machine learning is a set of methods that let computers learn patterns from data and improve at tasks without being explicitly programmed for every rule.
Malware
Software designed to harm or exploit systems.
Malware Classification
The process of labeling software as malicious or benign, often by file features, behavior, or sandbox traces. Machine learning improves accuracy and speed at scale.
Markdown Optimization
Setting discount timing and depth to clear inventory with the best margin. AI forecasts sell-through and recommends the most profitable markdown steps.
Market Microstructure
How trades are executed and prices are formed.
Mempool
The mempool holds unconfirmed transactions waiting to be added to a block. It affects ordering, fees, and attack surfaces like front-running and MEV.
Merkle Tree
A Merkle tree is a tree of hashes that summarizes many data items with a single root. It enables quick proofs that specific items are included without downloading all data.
MLOps
MLOps combines engineering and operations practices to move models from notebooks to production with reliability, monitoring, and continuous improvement.
Model Card
A standardized report that documents a model's purpose, data, performance, fairness considerations, limitations, and monitoring plans to support responsible deployment, stakeholder understanding, and ongoing governance.
Model Drift
When an AI model’s accuracy drops because data or user behavior changes over time, requiring monitoring and retraining.
Model Governance
Model governance sets controls to develop, validate, deploy, and monitor AI models safely, reducing bias, drift, and compliance risk across the model lifecycle.
Model Interpretability
The degree to which a person can understand how model inputs influence outputs, supporting trust, debugging, and responsible decision-making.
Model Lifecycle
The stages of building, deploying, and maintaining AI models.
Model Risk Management
Practices that ensure models are accurate, explainable, and controlled through validation, monitoring, and documentation.
Model Training
The process of teaching an AI model using data.
Monitoring
Tracking system health and performance over time.

N

Natural Language Processing
Natural language processing enables computers to understand, generate, and analyze human language, supporting chatbots, search, summarization, and document processing in business workflows.
Negative Prompting
A technique that specifies what the model should avoid, such as styles, objects, or tones. Common in image generation and text tasks to reduce artifacts, bias, and unwanted content.
Network Telemetry
Metrics and logs that describe network behavior.

O

OKR
A goal-setting method that links objectives to measurable results.
On-shelf Availability
Whether products are available for shoppers on the shelf.
One-Class SVM
A model trained only on normal data to find outliers.
Open Charge Point Protocol
An open protocol that manages communication between EV charging stations and central systems, enabling remote control, monitoring, billing, smart charging, and interoperability across multi-vendor charging networks.
Operating Model
How a team organizes people, process, and tech to deliver AI.
Operational Efficiency
Doing more with less effort, time, or cost.
Optical Character Recognition
Technology that converts images and PDFs of text into machine-readable data, enabling automated document workflows.
Oracle
An oracle supplies off-chain data to on-chain contracts, such as prices or weather. Secure oracle design prevents manipulation and minimizes trust in single data sources.

P

Patricia Trie
A Patricia trie is a compact key-value tree that uses shared prefixes to save space. With hashing, it provides verifiable state roots for fast lookups and proofs.
Persona-Based Prompting
A strategy that assigns the model a role, audience, and tone to shape style and decisions. It improves consistency for marketing, support, and teaching tasks across channels and formats.
Personalization
Delivering content or offers tailored to each user.
Personalized Learning
Personalized learning adapts content, pace, and support to each student’s needs and goals, often using data and AI-driven recommendations.
Phishing
A social engineering attack that tricks people into revealing information or installing malware. AI models spot phishing by analyzing text, headers, and URLs.
Phishing Detection
Using rules and machine learning to spot fake emails, messages, or sites that trick users. It analyzes text, links, and sender signals to classify risky content.
Planogram
A diagram showing where products should be placed on shelves to increase sales and improve shopper experience. AI helps optimize and check compliance automatically.
Planogram Compliance
Measuring how well in-store shelves match the approved planogram. AI and computer vision can detect exceptions and guide quick fixes.
Playbook Automation
Automating repetitive SOC workflows.
Portfolio Risk
Risk of loss across a basket of assets.
Power BI
A business intelligence tool for dashboards and reports.
Precision and Recall
Two metrics that show how well a model avoids false alarms (precision) and how many real cases it catches (recall).
Precision-Recall Curve
Shows trade-offs between precision and recall.
Prediction Accuracy
How often a model predicts the correct result.
Predictive Analytics
Methods that estimate future outcomes based on data patterns.
Price Elasticity
How demand changes when price changes. AI estimates elasticity by item, store, and time to guide smarter pricing and promotions.
Process Automation
Technology that automates repeatable business tasks.
Process Mining
Analyzing event logs to discover and optimize processes.
Promotion Uplift
The extra sales driven by a promotion beyond what would have happened anyway. AI estimates uplift and helps choose the best offers and timing.
Prompt Chaining
A workflow where outputs from one prompt feed into the next, breaking complex tasks into smaller steps for better control and higher quality.
Prompt Engineering
The practice of designing inputs that guide AI models to produce accurate, useful, and safe outputs for a task, using patterns, examples, constraints, and evaluation to improve results.
Prompt Evaluation Rubric
A structured scoring guide that rates prompt outputs against criteria like accuracy, completeness, style, and safety. Used to compare prompts, track changes, and guide iterative improvement.
Prompt Leakage
An information security risk where hidden instructions, system prompts, or proprietary context become exposed through outputs or attack prompts. It can harm safety, privacy, and competitive advantage.
Prompt Template
A reusable structure for prompts with placeholders for variables, examples, and constraints, designed to produce consistent outputs across similar tasks.
Public Key Cryptography
Public key cryptography uses a key pair: a public key for verification or encryption and a private key for signing or decryption. It enables secure identity and communication in open networks.

Q

Queue Detection
Using vision to monitor and manage checkout lines.

R

Recommendation System
AI that suggests products based on user behavior and context.
Reconciliation
Matching transactions across ledgers, statements, and systems to ensure balances agree and errors are resolved quickly.
Reentrancy
Reentrancy is a vulnerability where a contract’s external call lets another contract call back before state updates finish, enabling repeated withdrawals or logic misuse.
Regression
A model that predicts a numeric value.
Replenishment
Restocking products to maintain target inventory.
Responsible AI
Responsible AI means building and using AI systems that are safe, fair, transparent, and aligned with human values, with checks and accountability.
Retail KPIs
Key performance indicators for retail operations.
Retail Media
Advertising on a retailer’s owned properties.
Retail Voice AI
Voice interfaces for shopping and service in retail.
Retraining
Updating an AI model with new data to restore accuracy.
Retrieval-Augmented Generation
A technique that fetches relevant documents and adds them to the prompt, helping the model generate grounded, up-to-date, and verifiable responses.
Return on Investment
Return on investment measures the gain from an initiative compared to its cost. For AI, it blends cost savings, revenue lift, and risk reduction over time.
Risk Management
Risk management identifies, assesses, and controls potential harms. For AI, it covers model failures, bias, security, compliance, and operational risks.
Risk Scoring
Assigning a score that shows how risky a transaction, customer, or vendor is, to guide reviews and decisions.
Robotic Process Automation
Software that automates rules-based digital tasks.
ROC AUC
A measure of ranking quality across thresholds.
ROC Curve
A plot that shows the trade-off between true positive rate and false positive rate across thresholds. It helps compare models for detection tasks.
ROI (Return on Investment)
A measure of financial gain relative to cost.

S

Sandboxing
Running untrusted code in an isolated environment.
Scenario Planning
Exploring multiple possible futures to guide decisions.
Scoring Matrix
A grid to rank use cases by impact and feasibility.
Security Information and Event Management
Software that collects and correlates security events.
Security Operations Center
The team and tools that monitor and respond to threats.
Segmentation
Grouping items or customers by shared traits.
Sensitivity Analysis
Measuring how outputs change with inputs.
Sentiment Analysis
Using AI to score positive or negative tone in text.
SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)
A model-agnostic method that attributes a prediction to each feature using game theory, offering consistent and locally accurate explanations.
SKU
A unique identifier for a product item.
Smart Contract
A smart contract is code on a blockchain that runs automatically when rules are met. It enforces agreements without intermediaries, using transparent, tamper-resistant logic.
SMOTE
A method to balance classes by synthesizing samples.
Solidity
Solidity is a programming language for writing smart contracts, mainly on EVM-compatible blockchains. It looks like JavaScript and adds features for blockchain data, events, and access control.
Space Planning
Allocating shelf and floor space to products and categories to maximize sales and profit. AI balances demand, constraints, and shopper flow patterns.
Spear Phishing
Highly targeted phishing aimed at specific people.
Stakeholder Mapping
Identifying people affected by a project and their needs.
Static Analysis
Examining software artifacts without running them. Useful features include opcodes, imports, and strings for malware classification.
STIX
A standard format for sharing threat intel.
Store Analytics
Analysis of in-store traffic, sales, and operations.
Store Operations AI
Applying AI to daily store tasks like staffing, task management, and customer help. It boosts efficiency and consistency across locations.
Substation Configuration Language
An XML-based language in IEC 61850 for describing substation systems, IED capabilities, and communications. SCL files enable consistent engineering, interoperability, and testing across multi-vendor protection and control deployments.
Supervised Learning
A machine learning approach that trains models using labeled examples. It is common in phishing and malware classification where known good and bad samples guide learning.
Supply Chain Visibility
Seeing inventory and orders across suppliers, distribution, and stores. AI flags risks and delays so teams can act faster.
System Prompt
A high-priority instruction that sets role, tone, and boundaries for the model, shaping behavior across an entire conversation or workflow.

T

TAXII
A protocol to transport STIX data.
Teacher Workload Automation
Teacher workload automation uses AI to draft materials, organize tasks, and support grading, helping teachers focus more on instruction and feedback.
Text Classification
Assigning labels to text like spam, phishing, or benign.
Threat Intelligence
Curated information about adversaries, indicators, and tactics that helps defenders prioritize risks. AI enriches TI by clustering indicators and extracting entities from reports.
Time Series Forecasting
A method to predict future values, like revenue or cash, using historical data ordered over time. It helps finance teams plan demand, budgets, and inventory with trends and seasonality.
Time Series Modeling
Analyzing data over time to forecast the future. AI models capture trend, seasonality, and events for more accurate retail planning.
Time to Value
How quickly an AI initiative produces measurable benefits.
Tokenization
Splitting text into smaller pieces, like words or subwords, for modeling. It supports NLP tasks such as phishing classification and entity extraction from reports.
Total Cost of Ownership
The full cost of owning a system across its lifecycle.
Training Data
Training data is the labeled or structured information used to teach AI models. Its quality and coverage strongly influence accuracy, fairness, and reliability.
Transformer
A neural architecture built on attention mechanisms.
True Positive Rate
Fraction of real threats the model catches.

U

Unsupervised Learning
A learning method that finds patterns in unlabeled data. It supports anomaly detection and clustering indicators in threat intelligence enrichment.
Upgradeable Proxy
An upgradeable proxy lets a contract’s logic be replaced while keeping the same address and state, enabling fixes and improvements after deployment.
Uplift Modeling
Predicting the causal impact of an action on outcomes.
URL Reputation
A risk score that reflects how trustworthy a URL is.
Use Case
A defined business problem where AI can deliver value.

V

Value Chain
A set of activities that deliver a product or service.
Value Chain Analysis
A method to map activities that create value and cost.
Value Realization
The process of turning planned benefits into results.
Variance Analysis
Comparing planned results to actuals to find drivers.
Vector Database
A vector database stores numerical embeddings for fast similarity search, enabling retrieval-augmented generation and semantic search over documents and media.
Verifiable Random Function
A verifiable random function produces randomness tied to a public key, along with a proof that anyone can verify. It supports fair leader election and lotteries without trusted parties.
Visual Merchandising
Using product displays, signage, and layouts to encourage purchases and guide shoppers. AI tests layouts, predicts impact, and personalizes experiences by store.
Volt/VAR Control
A control strategy that manages voltage and reactive power on distribution feeders using inverter functions, capacitor banks, regulators, and setpoint curves to improve power quality, reduce losses, and support hosting capacity.

Z

Zero-Knowledge Proof
A zero-knowledge proof lets someone convince a verifier that a statement is true without revealing the underlying information. It enables privacy and compression in blockchain systems.
Zero-Shot Prompting
A prompting technique where the model is asked to perform a task without examples. Clear instructions, constraints, and role hints guide behavior when training data or examples are unavailable.
Zonal Analytics
Measuring performance by defined store areas.
