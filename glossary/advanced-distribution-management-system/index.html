Advanced Distribution Management System

A utility operations platform that integrates SCADA, DMS, and OMS to monitor and control distribution grids, optimize switching and voltage, and coordinate DER, AMI data, and customer programs in real time.

In detail

An Advanced Distribution Management System is the control platform for modern distribution operations. It integrates functions traditionally found in SCADA, Distribution Management Systems, and Outage Management Systems to deliver real-time situational awareness and control. Core capabilities include network modeling, state estimation, fault location isolation and service restoration, volt-var optimization, switching analysis, and work process coordination. ADMS ingests measurements from field devices and AMI, uses network connectivity models aligned with CIM, and interfaces with DERMS or IEEE 2030.5 servers to coordinate distributed energy resources. It provides operators with decision support, automation, and analytics that improve reliability and power quality while managing growing penetrations of renewables, storage, and EV charging. By standardizing integrations and data models, ADMS reduces operational silos and enables closed-loop control strategies that span from the substation to edge devices across feeders and microgrids.

Synonyms

ADMS platform, Distribution operations system, Integrated DMS and OMS

ADMS
