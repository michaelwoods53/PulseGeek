Confidence Interval

A range around a forecast that shows the uncertainty of predictions, helping plan for best and worst cases.

In detail

A confidence interval is a range that likely contains the true value of a forecasted metric, such as revenue or cash. Wider intervals mean more uncertainty. Finance teams use intervals to plan scenarios, set safety buffers, and communicate risk. Models should produce intervals that reflect data quality, volatility, and recent changes. Comparing intervals to actuals supports continuous improvement.

Synonyms

prediction interval
