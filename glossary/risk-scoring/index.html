Risk Scoring

Assigning a score that shows how risky a transaction, customer, or vendor is, to guide reviews and decisions.

In detail

Risk scoring converts features and signals into a single measure of risk, often on a scale or band. In finance, it is used for transaction monitoring, vendor risk, credit, and AML. Scores inform workflows like step-up verification, manual review, or automatic approval. AI can improve scores with nonlinear patterns and ensembles. Clear documentation, bias tests, and thresholds are important for governance and consistent decisions.

Synonyms

risk ranking, risk grading

Abbreviations

RS
