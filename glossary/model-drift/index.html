Model Drift

When an AI model’s accuracy drops because data or user behavior changes over time, requiring monitoring and retraining.

In detail

Model drift occurs when the relationship between inputs and outcomes changes, causing an AI system to make less accurate predictions. In finance, drift can come from seasonality shifts, new products, policy changes, or fraud tactics. Monitoring includes tracking metrics like precision, recall, MAPE, or AUC over time and comparing to baselines. Retraining, data quality checks, and feature reviews help recover performance. Documenting changes supports governance and audits.

Synonyms

concept drift, performance drift
