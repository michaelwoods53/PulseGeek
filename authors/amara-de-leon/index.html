Amara De Leon

Amara is the kind of person who notices the small frictions where technology meets everyday life—bus schedules optimized for no one, feeds that forget the quiet. She’s drawn to conversations at the edges: community forums, neighborhood libraries, late-night group chats where people ask what fairness really feels like. Long city walks and weekend trains are her favorite classrooms, and she collects overheard questions in a pocket notebook. She believes that ethics thrives in detail, in the stories people tell about what went wrong and what might be repaired. Food, music, and public spaces matter to her because they test whether inclusion is real. She gravitates toward open-source projects, local mutual-aid efforts, and art that makes systems visible. When things get noisy, she resets by tending a few plants and rereading a poem until the next better question arrives.
