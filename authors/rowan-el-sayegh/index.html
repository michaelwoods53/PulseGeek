Rowan El-Sayegh

Rowan loves the spark that appears when a complex idea finally lands, especially in rooms filled with curious minds. Afternoons often find her tinkering with learning tools, testing how small interface choices can change the way a student feels about a subject. She enjoys mentoring teens in community robotics clubs and hearing how they describe problems in their own words. Her worldview is shaped by multilingual conversations at home and the belief that education should feel like an invitation, not an obstacle course. She is fascinated by how quiet students communicate through projects, sketches, or code when given the right prompts. On weekends, she maps city walks to local libraries and makerspaces, treating each stop like a tiny laboratory for attention and wonder.
