Leena Narayanan

Leena moves through cities like a patient cartographer, noticing the way a crosswalk hums at dawn and how a pocket park gathers neighbors after sunset. She is fascinated by transit maps, district energy diagrams, and the quiet choreography between sensors and sidewalks. Weekends might find her cycling riverfront trails, chatting with street vendors about foot traffic, or timing the glow of adaptive streetlights as the evening crowd swells. She carries a small field camera to capture curb details, rain gardens, and rooftop solar reflections that tell bigger stories about care and resilience. Friends know her for impromptu neighborhood ‘shade walks’ in the summer, and for bringing spiced millet salad to block gatherings. She cares deeply about accessibility, elder-friendly design, and the small urban comforts—benches, water fountains, wayfinding—that make a city feel like a companion. At home, she tends herbs on a sunny sill and experiments with low-energy gadgets, always drawn to solutions that feel both elegant and practical.
