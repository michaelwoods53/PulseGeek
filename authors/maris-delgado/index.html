Maris Delgado

Maris delights in wandering grocery aisles at odd hours, noticing how endcaps whisper recommendations and how lighting nudges choices. She keeps a pocket notebook for small observations—barcode quirks, checkout rhythms, and the quiet choreography between staff and stock. City markets are her happy place, especially when technology hums softly in the background rather than shouting from the ceiling. She believes convenience and dignity should be twin pillars of modern commerce, with privacy as the lock on the door. When not studying storefronts, she experiments with tiny sensors and home automations, trying to make everyday routines quieter and kinder. Weekend rituals include neighborhood walks, tasting new teas, and sketching window displays that tell a story without a single word.
